№08 — Q&A things shop owners actually ask

Things shop owners ask.

I run a deburring / finishing shop, not a CNC shop. Will any of this fit?

Yes — that’s literally why FabTrack exists. The tool is built and run on the floor of a real deburring shop (SC Deburring LLC, Fresno CA). Stages are fully customizable, so “Pending → In Progress → Stamp → Wash → QC → Packing” can become “Inspect → Strip → Plate → Rinse → Seal → Pack” in 30 seconds. Process libraries, rework reason codes, and the QC flow were all designed by a finishing-shop owner who got tired of CNC-shaped software.

My operators barely touch a phone. How long to train them?

Operator side is intentionally tiny: punch in, see your jobs, punch out. Most floors are running unassisted by the second shift. We don’t hide anything important behind a menu — if it matters today, it’s on the screen. Owner side takes longer (an hour or two) because that’s where the quoting, reporting, and settings live.

Can my workflow be different per customer? Boeing wants different stages than Acme.

Yes. Per-customer routing is built in. You define the master stage list once, then per customer you can hide stages they don’t need or add steps they require. Job moves through the right stages automatically based on which customer ordered it. (Buried in Settings → Production → Customer Profiles right now — we’re moving it up front.)

How is the pricing really flat? What’s the catch?

No catch. One flat rate per shop, every operator on your floor punches in / sees jobs / logs time without counting toward a seat cap. Office / admin users are capped by tier (1 on Solo, 10 on Shop, unlimited on Scale). Floor operators are always free, on every tier.

What if my shop’s WiFi is garbage?

FabTrack installs as a PWA, so the screens you’ve already opened stay readable when WiFi drops — the operator can keep looking at their job list. Writes (clock-in, status changes) need a connection to land. A full offline write-queue is on the roadmap. If you have constant signal problems, talk to us before signing up — we’ll be honest about whether it’ll work.

Can I print job travelers and packing slips?

Yes. Job travelers print as PDF with a QR code on the header (scan it from your phone to jump back to the job). Packing slips print with line-item detail. Quotes and POs export as PDF too. All branded with your shop name, address, and logo.

Does this track delivery mileage for taxes?

Yes — GPS captures stops on a delivery run, calculates mileage automatically, and exports in IRS Publication 463 format (Date / Start Odo / End Odo / Miles / Purpose). Hand it straight to your CPA in April.

Do my customers need to make accounts to see their jobs?

No. They get a magic-link URL the first time you send them a portal invite (or you can put it in your email signature). One click, no password, no signup form. They see their active jobs, status, completed history, and any notes you’ve added. They cannot see your other customers.

How do I get my data OUT if I leave you?

Full export, anytime. Time logs export to Google Sheets in one click. Jobs, quotes, customers, vendors, POs, and rework entries export as CSV. Photos and PDFs download as a zip. Your data is yours — we don’t hold it hostage, and we don’t make leaving painful. (We’d rather earn the renewal.)

Phone, tablet, desktop, TV — which actually works?

All four, responsive from 375px to 4K. Most shops we see: operators on a shop-mounted iPad or their own phone, owner on a desktop for reporting, one Samsung mounted on the wall casting the Live Floor view. The TV mode is meant for 1080p+ — if you have an old 720p screen by the punch press, text gets tight.
